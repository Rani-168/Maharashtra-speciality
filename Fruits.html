1.Mango:


The mango, often referred to as the "king of fruits," is a tropical stone fruit native to South Asia, particularly India and Myanmar. It is known for its sweet, juicy flesh and distinctive aroma. Mangoes come in various varieties, each with its own unique flavor profile, ranging from tangy to sweet. They are rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, making them a popular choice for both consumption and culinary use. Mangoes are enjoyed fresh, in smoothies, desserts, and even savory dishes across the world.

2.Banana:


The banana is a widely consumed fruit that belongs to the genus Musa. It is characterized by its elongated shape, yellow peel when ripe, and soft, sweet flesh inside. Bananas are native to Southeast Asia but are now grown in many tropical and subtropical regions around the world. They are a rich source of essential nutrients, including potassium, vitamin C, and dietary fiber. Bananas are versatile and can be eaten on their own, added to smoothies, baked into goods, or used in various culinary dishes. They are also known for their natural energy-boosting properties.

3.Grapes


Grapes are small, round fruits that grow in clusters on vines and come in a variety of colors, including green, red, purple, and black. They are native to the Mediterranean region but are now cultivated in many parts of the world. Grapes can be eaten fresh as a snack, dried to make raisins, or processed to produce wine, juice, and jelly. They are rich in vitamins C and K, as well as antioxidants, which contribute to their health benefits. Grapes have a sweet flavor and a juicy texture, making them a popular choice for both eating and cooking.

4.Orange:


The orange is a citrus fruit known for its vibrant color, refreshing taste, and high vitamin C content. It is believed to have originated in Southeast Asia and is now cultivated in many parts of the world with suitable climates. Oranges are typically round and range in color from bright orange to yellow-orange. They are enjoyed fresh, juiced, or used in a variety of culinary applications, including desserts, salads, and sauces. In addition to being a delicious fruit, oranges are also valued for their health benefits, including boosting the immune system and providing antioxidants.
