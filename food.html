1.Vada Pav:


Vada Pav, often referred to as the Indian burger, is a popular street food from Maharashtra. It consists of a spicy potato fritter (vada) sandwiched between a soft bread roll (pav) and is typically served with tangy and spicy chutneys. It's a beloved snack known for its bold flavors and affordability.Vada pav, alternatively spelt wada pao,is a vegetarian fast food dish native to the Indian state of Maharashtra. The dish consists of a deep-fried potato dumpling placed inside a bread bun (pav) sliced almost in half through the middle. It is generally accompanied by one or more chutneys and a green chili pepper.Although it originated as an affordable street food in Mumbai, it is now served in food stalls and restaurants across India. It is also called Bombay burger in keeping with its origins and its resemblance in physical form to a burger.

2.Puran Poli:


Puran Poli is a traditional sweet flatbread from Maharashtra, typically prepared during festivals and special occasions. It is made by stuffing a mixture of chana dal (split chickpeas), jaggery (unrefined cane sugar), cardamom, and nutmeg into a wheat flour dough, which is then rolled out and cooked on a griddle. Puran Poli is cherished for its rich, sweet flavor and soft texture, often served with ghee (clarified butter) on top. 3.Modak:


Modak is a traditional sweet dumpling from Maharashtra, especially associated with the festival of Ganesh Chaturthi. It is made from rice flour or wheat flour dough filled with a mixture of grated coconut and jaggery, flavored with cardamom and sometimes garnished with nuts and raisins. Modaks can be steamed (ukadiche modak) or fried, and they are considered a favorite offering to Lord Ganesha. Their unique shape, resembling a teardrop or a conical form, adds to their charm and cultural significance. According to culinary historian Darra Goldstein, modaka is an ancient sweet that dates back to around 200 BCE.[6] Early mentions of modaka are found in Ayurveda, Ramayana and Mahabharata where it is described as a dumpling confectionery with sweet stuffing. Sangam literature similarly mentions modakas as rice dumplings filled with sweet stuffing that were also sold by street vendors in the ancient city of Madurai.[7][8] The medieval Manasollasa culinary text explains that modakas, as prepared with rice flour and a sweet stuffing with aromatic spices such as cardamom and camphor, were called Varsopalagolakas because they looked like hailstones.[9] Fried modakas are made with wheat flour, while steamed modakas are made from rice flour.

4.Misal Pav:


Misal Pav is a spicy and flavorful dish from Maharashtra, consisting of a spicy curry made from sprouted moth beans (matki) called "misal," served with soft bread rolls known as "pav." The misal is typically topped with farsan (a crunchy snack mix), chopped onions, coriander, and a squeeze of lemon juice. This dish is known for its bold and spicy flavors, making it a popular breakfast or snack option in Maharashtra. 5.Pav Bhaji:


Pav Bhaji is a popular street food dish from Maharashtra, consisting of a spicy vegetable mash (bhaji) served with buttered and toasted bread rolls (pav). The bhaji is made by cooking a variety of vegetables like potatoes, tomatoes, peas, and bell peppers with a blend of spices, resulting in a flavorful and aromatic dish. Pav Bhaji is typically garnished with chopped onions, cilantro, and a squeeze of lemon juice, making it a beloved and satisfying meal enjoyed by people of all ages.
